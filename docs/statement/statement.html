Artist Statement

Domestic items are an emotional exploration of what is left behind.

With my work I concentrate on the representation of the tangible, here

and now, that can trigger a feeling or a memory.

Works like “Mi Mesa” place me in at a happy family dinner, “My Kitchen”

depicts the utensils we use when cooking together in any family

gathering. The editions of “mi mate” bring me closer to my friends and my

Heritage.

I work in printmaking. Producing multiples makes me think I am generating connections. People drawn by the same picture

but engaging with it in different ways. A connection they don’t know

Exists.

My preferred technique is relief. Carving on wood or linoleum is almost

meditative, I can feel what I am doing. With each piece of wood removed I

uncover more of me. When rolling the ink on a plate I see the full image

becoming a reality. Then I pass it through the press and slowly and,

somewhat anxiously, I peel up the paper to unveil my final work. Even

though these are repetitive movements I feel the excitement, surprise and

relief with every print.

I am currently experimenting with collage, adding texture to my pieces

through Chine collé, knitted items, fabric and stitches.

Mostly I bring portions of my previous printings into a new composition. I

feel I am creating a collection of memories, or perhaps a glimpse into the

future.
